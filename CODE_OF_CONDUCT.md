# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We want Sterling to be a welcoming, curious place for people who care about data, design, writing, and open source. Participation is expected to be respectful across experience level, identity, discipline, and communication style.

## Expected behavior

- Be kind, specific, and constructive.
- Critique ideas, implementations, and evidence—not people.
- Credit sources, collaborators, and prior work.
- Assume good faith, ask clarifying questions, and leave room for different technical and editorial traditions.
- Respect requests around private, sensitive, or governed data.

## Unacceptable behavior

Harassment, discriminatory language, personal attacks, intimidation, deliberate misinformation, sharing private information, or sustained disruption of discussion are not welcome.

## Enforcement

Report conduct concerns privately to [@LaMatemaga](https://github.com/LaMatemaga). Reports will be handled with discretion. Depending on context, responses may range from a request to change behavior to removal from project spaces.

This Code of Conduct applies in issues, pull requests, discussions, and any project-related public interaction.

Adapted from the [Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/), version 2.1.
